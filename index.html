Evening Build

Contemplating the relationship between community, technology, and repair

What This Is

Evening Build is about contemplating the relationship between community, technology, and repair. We gather monthly to ask: Is it possible to fix what's been wrought onto us?

This isn't a networking event or a masterclass. It's more like a themed group chat that brings the right mix of people into rooms at the right times—then lets the magic happen in the margins. Some months we'll discuss a book. Other times we'll invite a guest or just share a meal together.

If you worry about algorithms deciding vulnerable people's healthcare, if you've watched tech reshape your neighborhood without your input, if you think we need to look both ways before crossing rather than writing off harm as collateral damage—this might be for you.

What We Want to Do

Build Community Nodes

We're not building software. We're building connections between people who care about consequences, sustainability, and repair in our technical systems.

Create Space for Repair

Moving beyond "move fast and break things" toward maintenance, thoughtfulness, and looking both ways before we cross.

Foster Real Conversation

Bringing together people who worry about algorithmic healthcare decisions AND have pet chinchillas named Douglass.

Practice Community Care

Engaging with our neighbors as humans, not faces we can sell stuff to. Learning from Nordic approaches to civic engagement.

The Format

Evening Build adapts to what the moment needs. Sometimes we'll examine a book together. Other times we'll have a guest share their work. Maybe we'll try a workshop or simply share a meal and see what emerges.

Food is nearby, music stays low, and the real conversations happen in the margins. We're committing to three initial events to see if this resonates.

You probably won't leave with a best friend, but you might meet someone you didn't know before. Maybe you'll see them again and it'll be warm because you'll remember they worry about the same things you do.

Stay Updated

We'll announce our first event soon. For now, this is an experiment in building something worth showing up for.
